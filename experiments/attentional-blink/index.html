Background

The attentional blink (Raymond, Shapiro & Arnell, 1992) reveals a striking temporal limitation of visual attention. When two targets are embedded in a rapid serial visual presentation (RSVP) stream, the second target (T2) is often missed if it appears 200–500 ms after the first target (T1).

Key Features

  • The blink window: T2 detection drops dramatically at lags 2–5 (~200–500 ms)
  • Lag-1 sparing: Paradoxically, T2 is often not missed at lag 1 (immediately after T1)
  • Recovery: T2 detection returns to near-ceiling by lag 7–8

This Experiment

You will see a rapid stream of letters. T1 is a white letter on a black background (among black letters on white). T2 is the letter “X”. After each stream, report T1’s identity and whether you saw an X.

References

  1. Raymond, J.E., Shapiro, K.L., & Arnell, K.M. (1992). Temporary suppression of visual processing in an RSVP task: An attentional blink? Journal of Experimental Psychology: HPP, 18, 849–860.
  2. Chun, M.M. & Potter, M.C. (1995). A two-stage model for multiple target detection in RSVP. Journal of Experimental Psychology: HPP, 21, 109–127.
